# YouTube Channel — Project Context & Topic Research

## What We're Building

An **automated documentary YouTube channel** in the style of Curious Mind, Wendover Productions, and Vox Explainers. The content is explainer/informational documentary — not talking-head, not opinion. Think: narrated, cinematic, B-roll-heavy, topic-driven.

### The Pipeline (technical)

- **Narration**: Generated via ElevenLabs
- **Sync timing**: Whisper (word-level timestamps) used to lock cuts to narration
- **B-roll + main footage**: Everglades footage was the first test bed; model now applies to any topic
- **Edit versions built so far**:
  - v1 — simple cinematic B-roll cut
  - v2 — early narration sync attempt
  - v3–v4 — whisper-locked timelines, sync-fixed milestone at v4
  - v5–v6 — semantic clip matching from a per-second main-video catalog; v6 is current best
- **v6 features**: hard cuts locked to narration, topic-aware clip selection, fewer repeated frames
- **Sync audit tools**: score each shot against the narration, catch mismatches
- **Thumbnail style**: clicky two-line hooks e.g. "Miami's dirty secret / you have no idea…"
- **Title + description style**: Curious Mind formula — emotional hook + informational payoff

### Content Format

- Style: explainer, informational, documentary narration
- Length: long-form (YouTube main feed, not Shorts)
- No talking head — fully narrated with B-roll
- Strong thumbnail + title is critical (competing in curiosity/mystery niche)
- Each video has a "hidden truth" or "you didn't know this" angle

### 🌍 Geopolitics

#### 1. China owns the minerals inside every device you own
- **The story**: China controls the entire rare earth supply chain — mining, refining, and magnet-making. The West has almost no alternative. It's the oil crisis of the digital age.
- **Why now**: Exploding in investment and policy circles. A REalloys CEO said in May 2026: *"It used to be oil and OPEC. It's now China against everybody."*
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"The material in your phone is a weapon / and only one country has it"`
- **B-roll**: mines, circuit boards, EV batteries, maps of China supply chain, Pentagon footage

#### 2. Iran is building a nuclear bomb — and nobody can stop it
- **The story**: The US bombed Iran's nuclear facilities in June 2025, but the program is already reviving. The last US–Russia arms treaty (New START) expired in February 2026. Nuclear fear is back.
- **Why now**: Scientific American named this a top 2026 story. High tension, high search interest.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"The world's most dangerous countdown / started again last year"`
- **B-roll**: centrifuges, missile launches, UN Security Council footage, timeline graphics

#### 3. Greenland is now the most strategically important island on Earth
- **The story**: US, China, Denmark, and Canada are all quietly competing for Greenland — rare earths, a melting Arctic shipping route, and a nuclear launch corridor above North America.
- **Why now**: Trump's Greenland push in 2025 revived global interest; Arctic ice loss is accelerating.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"A forgotten island is the center of the next world war"`
- **B-roll**: Arctic landscape, maps, rare earth mining, military bases, shipping lanes

### 🌱 Environment

#### 4. The glacier that could drown Miami
- **The story**: West Antarctica's Thwaites "Doomsday Glacier" ice shelf is approaching collapse. Scientists say it alone could raise sea levels by several feet — enough to reshape Florida's coastline permanently.
- **Why now**: Ice shelf breach warnings were reported in May 2026. Direct Florida/Miami relevance for the channel's home base.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"Florida has 15 years / engineers know it, politicians don't"`
- **B-roll**: aerial glacier footage, Miami flooding maps, sea level rise animations, scientists drilling ice cores

#### 5. The hidden water war nobody is talking about
- **The story**: Corporations are quietly buying up the world's freshwater sources. Rivers on four continents are running dry. A new 21st-century resource war is already underway.
- **Why now**: A DW documentary on this went viral; water privatization is a rising global story.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"Water is being privatized / you don't own a drop"`
- **B-roll**: dry riverbeds, water treatment plants, protest footage, rivers at risk

#### 6. The coral reefs are dying in real time — and it's accelerating
- **The story**: 2025 was the first year ocean temperatures breached 1.5°C for a full 12 months. Mass bleaching events are now annual. Some reefs may not exist by 2035.
- **Why now**: WMO confirmed the 1.5°C breach; Chasing Coral (Netflix) created huge awareness baseline.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"Half the ocean's color is gone / this is what's left"`
- **B-roll**: bleached coral footage, underwater cameras, before/after reef comparisons

### 🔬 Science

#### 7. Microplastics are inside your brain right now
- **The story**: 2024–26 research found microplastics in human hearts, lungs, blood, and brain tissue. Nobody fully understands what they're doing there yet.
- **Why now**: Multiple peer-reviewed papers + the documentary *Plastic People* (2024) built audience awareness. Search volume still climbing.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"Scientists found plastic in a human brain / and yours has it too"`
- **B-roll**: lab microscope footage, food packaging, ocean plastic, human anatomy graphics

#### 8. How a new space telescope will rewrite everything we know
- **The story**: NASA's Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope launches autumn 2026. It has 100x the field of view of Hubble and is expected to challenge current dark matter theory.
- **Why now**: Launch window confirmed for late 2026 — perfect timing for a pre-launch explainer.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"The most powerful eye ever built / launches this year"`
- **B-roll**: telescope assembly footage, deep space imagery, dark matter graphics, Hubble comparisons

### 💻 Tech / AI

#### 9. AI is secretly draining your city's water supply
- **The story**: Google, Microsoft, and Amazon data centers use millions of gallons of local water daily for cooling. Cities like Phoenix and Mexico City are already running short.
- **Why now**: Water use reports are causing backlash in 2025–26; directly connects AI hype to a tangible hidden cost.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"Every ChatGPT message costs a bottle of water / here's the math"`
- **B-roll**: data center exterior + cooling towers, desert cities, water meters, server racks

#### 10. The energy grid is not ready for AI — and the clock is running out
- **The story**: AI data center power demand grew 72% year-over-year in 2025. Power grids built in the 1970s cannot handle it. Blackout risks are rising across the US South and Midwest.
- **Why now**: Hyperscaler capex hit ~$400 billion in 2025; grid stress reports are multiplying.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"AI wants 10x more power than we have / who turns the lights off?"`
- **B-roll**: power lines, transformer stations, data center rows, US grid maps, outage footage

### 🏙️ Society

#### 11. America's pipes are rotting — and nobody has the money to fix it
- **The story**: US wastewater and water treatment infrastructure is crumbling. Forever chemicals (PFAS), aging equipment, and gutted FEMA funding mean the next failure could be catastrophic.
- **Why now**: FEMA downsizing + aging infra = a viral documentary niche in 2026. The film *Unless Something Goes Terribly Wrong* (2025) proved the appetite.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"The pipes under your city are 100 years old / one crack changes everything"`
- **B-roll**: wastewater plants, corroded pipes, flooded neighborhoods, PFAS chemical graphics

#### 12. The US public health system is flying blind
- **The story**: CDC layoffs and data system cuts mean the US can no longer reliably track disease outbreaks, overdose deaths, or biosecurity threats. The next pandemic would catch everyone off guard.
- **Why now**: Scientific American flagged this as a top 2026 story. Post-COVID audience is primed for this.
- **Thumbnail hook**: `"The system built to save you / was quietly dismantled"`
- **B-roll**: empty CDC offices, disease tracking dashboards, hospital hallways, policy documents
